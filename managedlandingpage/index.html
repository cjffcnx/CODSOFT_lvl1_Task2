The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China, built between the 3rd century BC and the 17th century AD, is an iconic structure stretching over 13,000 miles (20,000 kilometers) across northern China. It served as a defense barrier, constructed using materials like stone, brick, and packed earth. This engineering marvel, built by countless laborers over centuries, stands as a symbol of China rich history and architectural ingenuity

The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a beautiful white marble monument in Agra, India. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away. The Taj Mahal is famous for its stunning architecture, with a large dome, detailed carvings, and beautiful gardens. It is one of the most famous landmarks in the world and a symbol of love

Lumbini

Lumbini, located in Nepal, is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who became the Buddha. It is an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists worldwide. The site features ancient temples, including the Maya Devi Temple, and the sacred Bodhi Tree. Many countries, such as India, Japan, Thailand, and South Korea, have contributed to the development and restoration of Lumbini by building monasteries and supporting preservation efforts, making it a center of peace and spirituality

The Great Pyramid of Gaza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the largest Egyptian pyramid. It served as the tomb of pharaoh Khufu, who ruled during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom. Built c. 2600 BC, over a period of about 26 years, the pyramid is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World
